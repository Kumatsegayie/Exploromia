People and Heritage

The Oromo people carry rich cultural systems, oral traditions, and identity rooted in land and community.

  • Gadaa system and civic organization
  • Oral tradition, songs, and storytelling
  • Religious and spiritual practice
  • Textile, craft, dance, and music expressions
  • Customary laws and conflict resolution
  • Pastoral and agricultural knowledge

About Exploromia

Exploromia showcases Oromia's beauty and Oromo heritage through modern, clear digital storytelling.

The project supports cultural visibility, responsible tourism, and better global awareness of Oromia.

Built by contributors from Lead Star International Academy, including Kuma Tsegaye, Yosen Tadele, and Yisehak Tibeso.

Our aim is to document places, stories, and traditions in a way that is respectful to local communities and useful for global visitors, students, and researchers.

Mission

Promote Oromo identity and history globally.

Sustainability

Encourage ethical travel and heritage protection.

Vision

Link local culture and global understanding.

What We Do

  • Curate destination highlights with context, not only images.
  • Preserve cultural narratives through accessible digital pages.
  • Connect youth contributors, developers, and cultural advocates.
  • Keep one simple platform that is easy to use on mobile devices.

Why It Matters

  • It challenges misinformation with authentic local perspective.
  • It supports pride in Oromo heritage and intergenerational learning.
  • It encourages ethical tourism that benefits local communities.
  • It helps new audiences discover Oromia beyond stereotypes.
1. Research and Collection Gather references, community stories, and site information from trusted local inputs.
2. Content Design and Verification Transform raw information into clear, structured, and respectful storytelling.
3. Publication and Iteration Release updates, collect feedback, and continuously improve quality and coverage.
